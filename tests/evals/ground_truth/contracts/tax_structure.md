# Tax Structure Memorandum

**Prepared for:** Company A Board of Directors
**Date:** March 1, 2024

## Section 1 — Corporate Structure

1.1 Company A is incorporated in Delaware, USA, with operating subsidiaries
in Ontario (Canada), Dublin (Ireland), and Singapore.

1.2 The Irish subsidiary holds licensing rights for EMEA customers and
recognizes royalty income under a cost-sharing arrangement with the US parent.

## Section 2 — Transfer Pricing

2.1 Intercompany services are priced at cost-plus 8%, benchmarked against
a 2022 transfer pricing study covering years 2020-2022.

2.2 The transfer pricing study has not been updated for FY2023. OECD
guidelines recommend triennial updates; the current study is overdue.

2.3 The Irish subsidiary pays a 5% royalty on EMEA net revenue to the US
parent. This rate was set in 2019 and has not been revisited.

## Section 3 — Tax Positions

3.1 Company A claims an R&D tax credit (SR&ED) in Canada of approximately
CAD 450,000 for FY2023. Supporting documentation consists of project time
logs and invoices from external contractors.

3.2 The US entity has accumulated Net Operating Losses (NOLs) of approximately
USD 2.1M, subject to Section 382 limitations upon a change of control.

3.3 The Singapore entity benefits from a Pioneer Certificate granting a 5%
concessionary tax rate through 2026, contingent on maintaining local headcount
above 15 employees.

## Section 4 — Indirect Taxes

4.1 Company A does not currently collect sales tax in 7 US states where it
has remote employees, despite potential nexus under Wayfair.

4.2 VAT registrations are active in Germany, France, and the UK. The Irish
subsidiary handles EU VAT on SaaS revenue.

## Section 5 — Pending Matters

5.1 Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) is auditing FY2022 SR&ED claims. No
assessment has been issued. Management estimates exposure at CAD 120,000.

5.2 No tax litigation is pending in any jurisdiction.

## Section 6 — Post-Acquisition Considerations

6.1 Section 382 NOL limitation: annual usage capped at approximately
USD 350,000 post-acquisition (based on long-term tax-exempt rate × equity value).

6.2 Pioneer Certificate in Singapore may be revoked if headcount drops
below 15 during integration workforce rationalization.

6.3 Transfer pricing arrangements should be renegotiated to reflect
post-acquisition economic substance.
