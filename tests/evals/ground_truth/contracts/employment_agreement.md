# Employment Agreement

**Between:** Company A ("Employer") and Jane Smith ("Employee")
**Effective Date:** January 15, 2024

## Section 1 — Position and Duties

1.1 Employee is hired as Senior Software Engineer, reporting to the VP of
Engineering.

1.2 Employee's primary work location is the Employer's office in Toronto,
Ontario, with remote work permitted 3 days per week.

## Section 2 — Compensation

2.1 Base salary: CAD 185,000 per annum, paid bi-weekly.

2.2 Annual performance bonus: target 15% of base salary, subject to individual
and company performance metrics reviewed by the compensation committee.

2.3 Equity: 10,000 stock options vesting over 4 years with a 1-year cliff.

## Section 3 — Benefits

3.1 Employee is eligible for the group health benefits plan after 90 days.

3.2 Employer provides 20 days paid vacation, 5 personal days, and 10 sick days
per calendar year.

## Section 4 — Non-Competition and Non-Solicitation

4.1 For 24 months following termination, Employee shall not engage in any
business that competes directly with Employer within Canada.

4.2 For 18 months following termination, Employee shall not solicit any
employee or contractor of Employer.

## Section 5 — Intellectual Property

5.1 All inventions, works, and discoveries made during employment and related
to Employer's business are the sole property of Employer.

5.2 Employee agrees to assign all rights in such inventions to Employer.

## Section 6 — Termination

6.1 Employer may terminate without cause by providing 8 weeks written notice
or pay in lieu thereof, plus statutory minimums under the Ontario Employment
Standards Act.

6.2 Upon termination, unvested stock options are forfeited. Vested options
remain exercisable for 90 days post-termination.

## Section 7 — Change of Control

7.1 In the event of a change of control, Employee's unvested options
shall accelerate and vest in full (single-trigger acceleration).

7.2 If Employee is terminated within 12 months of a change of control,
Employee is entitled to 12 months severance (double-trigger protection).

## Section 8 — Governing Law

This agreement is governed by the laws of the Province of Ontario, Canada.
