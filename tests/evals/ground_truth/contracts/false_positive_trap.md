# Employee Handbook — Technology Policies

**Company:** Company A
**Effective Date:** January 15, 2024
**Version:** 4.2

## Section 1 — Introduction

This handbook outlines Company A's policies regarding technology use, data
handling, and employee conduct. All employees must acknowledge receipt of
this handbook within 30 days of their start date.

## Section 2 — Acceptable Use

2.1 Company devices are provided for business purposes. Limited personal use
is permitted during breaks and non-working hours.

2.2 Employees must not install unauthorized software on company devices.

2.3 Employees must not bypass security controls or share credentials.

## Section 3 — Employee Termination Procedures

3.1 Upon termination of employment (voluntary or involuntary), the employee
must return all company devices within 5 business days.

3.2 IT will disable the terminated employee's access to all systems within
4 hours of the termination notification from HR.

3.3 The terminated employee's manager must review and reassign any
outstanding work items within 10 business days of the termination date.

3.4 Exit interviews are conducted by HR for all voluntary terminations.

## Section 4 — Data Classification

4.1 Company A classifies data into four tiers:
- **Public**: Marketing materials, press releases
- **Internal**: Org charts, internal memos, operational data tables
- **Confidential**: Customer lists, financial projections, strategic plans
- **Restricted**: PII, credentials, encryption keys

4.2 Data tables and spreadsheets containing only Internal-tier information
may be shared via the company intranet without additional approval.

## Section 5 — Password Policy

5.1 Passwords must be at least 12 characters with mixed case, numbers, and
special characters.

5.2 Passwords must be changed every 90 days.

5.3 Password reuse is prohibited for the last 10 passwords.

## Section 6 — Remote Work

6.1 Remote work requires VPN connection to the corporate network.

6.2 Employees must use company-provided laptops for remote work.

6.3 Home office environments must be free from unauthorized observers during
calls involving confidential information.

## Section 7 — Violations

Violations of these policies may result in disciplinary action up to and
including termination of employment.
